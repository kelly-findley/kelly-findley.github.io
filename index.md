# Kelly Findley, PhD

Hi, and welcome to my page! I'm a recent grad of Florida State University, where I completed a PhD in Math Education and a Masters in Statistics. Beginning in Fall 2019, I'll be working at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign as a Teaching Assistant Professor in the Statistics Department. My teaching and research interests are primarily in statistics education. 

Working with graduate teaching assistants and instructors is one of my highest priorities. Being a graduate student is already new and stressful enough, and as a result, teaching is often thrown by the wayside as a gig to pay the bills. But I believe that teaching can be an enriching experience and highlight of any graduate degree program! It certainly was for me. My philosophy toward TA training is to create a supportive and ambitious TA community that encourages participants to develop clear, personal goals, identify and overcome common barriers and concerns, and develop a unique vision for teaching introductory statistics courses. Central to this philosophy is immersing TAs in data investigations and interesting activities that they can also facilitate with their own students. Departments often assume that TAs have all the content knowledge they need to teach, but to the contrary, new TAs often don't have a well-developed conceptual understanding of the material and may not have a solid curricular vision for intro. If you are interested in learning more about research on TA training and support in statistics or mathematics, reach out to me!
